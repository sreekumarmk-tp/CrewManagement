# Seafarer Repatriation Rules (MLC 2006)

Apply these rules when deciding whether and how to arrange a crew member's
sign-off travel. They are based on the Maritime Labour Convention, 2006 (MLC),
Regulation 2.5 and Standard A2.5.

> This is a practical summary for routing/eligibility decisions, not legal advice.
> Where the seafarer's employment agreement (SEA) or flag-state law is more
> generous, the more favourable terms apply.

## When repatriation is an entitlement

A seafarer is entitled to repatriation (at no cost to them) when:

- The employment agreement **expires** while abroad, or is terminated by either the
  shipowner or the seafarer for justified reasons.
- The seafarer can **no longer perform duties** under the agreement (illness, injury).
- The vessel is **sold, lost, or re-flagged**, or the voyage to a war zone the
  seafarer doesn't consent to.

Maximum continuous service period before repatriation is due: typically **no more
than 11 months** on board.

## Who bears the cost

- The **shipowner bears the cost** of repatriation in entitlement cases above.
- Cost may be recovered from the seafarer **only** where they are found in serious
  default of their obligations (per the SEA / national law). Do not assume default —
  treat travel as shipowner-funded by default.

## Destination

Repatriate to one of, per the seafarer's choice in the SEA:
- the place of **engagement**,
- the place named in the **collective agreement**, or
- the seafarer's **country of residence / home country**.

Default to the **home country** when the SEA is silent. Route travel accordingly
(see crew-travel-policy).

## Entitlements that travel out of scope but should be flagged

Repatriation generally includes passage, accommodation and meals in transit, and
reasonable baggage allowance. If the itinerary forces an overnight layover, flag
that transit accommodation is required.

## Output

State the repatriation basis (e.g. "contract completion"), the funding party
(default: shipowner), and the chosen destination. If any condition suggests the
seafarer is **not** entitled (rare), flag it for Shore Manager review rather than
deciding unilaterally.
