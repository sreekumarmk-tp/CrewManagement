# Visa & Transit Requirements

Use this when selecting a routing in `generateTicket`. A booking is only valid if
the crew member can legally enter or transit **every** airport on the itinerary.
Prefer routes that need the fewest visas.

> Requirements change frequently and depend on nationality, the specific airport,
> and whether the crew member leaves the international transit area. Treat the
> guidance below as defaults and flag anything uncertain for the Shore Manager /
> manning agent to confirm.

## General principle

- A **direct flight** to the home country usually avoids transit-visa issues — prefer it.
- Airside transit (not passing immigration) often needs no visa, but several
  countries require a transit visa even airside for certain nationalities.

## Common seafarer / transit visa regimes

| Hub / Country | Watch out for |
|---|---|
| **Schengen area** (Frankfurt, Amsterdam, Paris…) | Airport Transit Visa (ATV) required for several nationalities even when airside. Seafarers may use a **Schengen seaman transit visa** with a letter of guarantee. |
| **United States** (any US connection) | No airside transit — all passengers clear immigration. Crew need a **C-1/D** crew/transit visa; without it, **avoid US routings entirely**. |
| **United Kingdom** | Direct Airside Transit Visa (DATV) required for many nationalities; a **Transit Visa** if landside. |
| **Gulf hubs** (Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi) | Generally transit-friendly; long layovers may need a transit visa (often sponsored by the airline). |
| **China** | 24/72/144-hour visa-free transit only at specific airports and for eligible nationalities — verify before routing. |

## Documents to assume the crew member carries

- Valid passport (check ≥ 6 months validity), **Seafarer's Identity Document / CDC**,
  the **SEA**, and the **letter of guarantee** from the shipowner/agent for transit.

## Decision rule

1. Try a direct flight home first.
2. If a connection is needed, choose a hub whose transit rules the crew member's
   nationality satisfies (or where the airline sponsors the transit visa).
3. **Never** route through the US without confirmed C-1/D.
4. If no clearly compliant routing exists, propose the best candidate and **flag for
   manning-agent confirmation** rather than guaranteeing it.

## Output

State the routing, each transit point, and the visa basis for transiting it (e.g.
"airside, no visa required" / "Schengen seaman transit visa + letter of guarantee").
